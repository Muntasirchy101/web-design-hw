What Exactly Does a Virtual Assistant Do?

A virtual assistant, or VA, is an independent contractor or freelancer usually hired to assist with daily administrative duties and other business services. They're ideal if you're running a business solo, especially when the workload becomes overwhelming and time-consuming, taking away opportunities for you to build and grow. A VA is sort of like a personal assistant, but focuses more on general company tasks.

Virtual assistants work remotely, and responsibilities will vary depending on the skills they offer and the needs of your business. Typically, VAs will assist with general tasks that do not require high-level training or knowledge. Here are some of the most common:

  • Data entry
  • Scheduling appointments
  • Managing email accounts
  • Online research
  • Social media management
  • Creating presentations
  • Bookkeeping
  • File organization
  • Customer service
  • Lead generation

Sometimes, virtual assistants are qualified for more specialized services like graphic design, web development, and marketing. If you don't have the skills or time, you can find a virtual assistant who can handle these necessary tasks. Remember that if you hire a VA for creative work, you'll want to ensure they have the time to complete the more tedious tasks.

Why Should You Hire a Virtual Assistant?

There are a ton of ways that you can benefit by hiring a virtual assistant. Outsourcing tasks can make a huge difference in the success of your business and is an excellent move for a start-up. It'll add value to your business without investing in additional staff, making it more cost-effective. They can also help you stay organized and track deadlines and important dates, so you never miss a beat.

You also won't have to worry about outfitting an office space and supplies. You can employ a worker from anywhere at any time and even hire multiple virtual assistants always to have someone available. Most importantly, it frees up much-needed time to focus on what matters the most- growth and profitability.

How To Hire a VA

Before hiring a virtual assistant, it's essential to determine a few key things to help you find a match that best suits your business. First, you'll need to define what you need them to do and how much you're willing to pay. Next, you'll want to outline what qualifies a virtual assistant to work specifically for your business just as you would a full-time employee. Factors such as language, location, professional experience, and the available budget are essential to consider.

Once you find a suitable virtual assistant, it's vital to define your expectations and communication methods clearly. If you have to constantly reiterate their responsibilities and your needs, it defeats the purpose, right? Micromanaging could take up as much time as handling the work yourself. Clear communication upfront will help ensure a successful working relationship.
